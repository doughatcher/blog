This isn't a story about a vulnerability in your store. Your store is fine.

It's a story about what happens when someone builds a convincing replica of your brand that your customers can't distinguish from the real thing — and what it costs you when they find out later.

Security researchers published findings this week on a skimming operation called GorgonAgora: 4,800+ fake online storefronts built to impersonate real brands. The list includes Starbucks, Nike, Disney, Ford, Sony, Lego and hundreds more. The fake stores carry the same products, same imagery, same checkout flow. The difference is where the payment data goes.

## The customer trust problem

Your customer finds a link, lands on a site that looks exactly like your brand, completes a purchase. Their card gets stolen. They contact your support team. They dispute the charge as fraud. They post about it. They don't shop with you again.

None of this happened on your infrastructure. But the trust they extended was trust in your brand — and that trust is now damaged.

That's what CVE numbers don't capture. The attack surface isn't your checkout security. It's your brand's presence anywhere on the internet. You can harden your own checkout and still have this problem, because the attack never touches your systems.

## What to do right now

GorgonAgora has been active since August 2025 and is still expanding. Three concrete steps:

1. Search for your brand on urlscan.io — the security community has published a fingerprint identifying these stores. See if any are impersonating you.
2. Set up trademark monitoring for .shop domains and common impersonation patterns (your brand + "official," "store," "hub," "direct").
3. Reinforce your canonical checkout URL in customer-facing communications — receipts, shipping confirmations, marketing email. Make it easy for customers to know what your real domain looks like.

This is the Post-Launch Reality: your checkout security is one piece. The broader surface is everywhere your name appears.
